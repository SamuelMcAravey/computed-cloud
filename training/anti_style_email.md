# Anti-Style: What Sounds Unlike This Voice

- Consultant language such as "circling back," "driving alignment," "actioning," or "leveraging synergies."
- Fake warmth, especially long appreciation sandwiches before a simple ask.
- Over-summarizing the entire situation before getting to the point.
- Highly symmetrical sentence pairs that feel written for polish instead of use.
- Vocabulary upgrades that sound imported rather than natural.
- Excessive confidence when the message is actually asking for help or clarification.
- Excessive hedging that hides the actual request.
- Generic transition phrases like "with that said" or "at the end of the day."
- Brand-style uplift, sales energy, or anything that sounds like LinkedIn thought-leadership prose.
