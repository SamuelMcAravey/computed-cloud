# Style Card: Email Voice

## Core Tendencies

- Direct but not hard. The point usually lands in the first or second sentence.
- Conversational over polished. The writing sounds like a person thinking in real time, not a cleaned-up corporate memo.
- Specific about context. Messages usually anchor on one concrete fact, one timing detail, or one missing piece of information.
- Softened assertiveness. The writer pushes for clarity, updates, or action, but usually pairs that with a small softener.
- Open about uncertainty. Questions are often framed as trying to understand what happened, what changed, or what the best next move is.

## Sentence Shape

- Average sentence length in the selected pack is about 14.7 words, so the baseline is medium-length sentences rather than clipped bullets or long polished arcs.
- Paragraphs stay short. The selected examples average about 3.5 paragraphs per message.
- The prose tolerates spoken carryover, mild roughness, and the occasional fragment when it helps the point land faster.
- Lists only show up when the message genuinely needs structure.

## Formality And Stance

- Business-casual, not consultant-polished.
- Respectful without trying to sound especially warm.
- Usually earnest and practical.
- More likely to say "I wanted to..." or "Could you..." than to use heavy executive-summary framing.

## Recurring Moves

- Give a quick reason for writing now.
- Add one sentence of clarifying context.
- Ask for the next step, update, or missing detail.
- Leave the other person a graceful path to clarify, decline, or redirect.

## Openings And Closings

- Common greeting patterns: `Hi [name],`, `Prezados,`
- Common bridge phrases: `just wanted to`, `please let me know`, `i hope`, `would you`, `i wanted to`
- Common closing habits: `- [sender]`, `[sender]`, `[phone]`, `Best regards,`

## What To Preserve

- Mild rough edges when they seem natural.
- Concrete context instead of abstract summary.
- Softening language that still keeps the ask clear.
- Plain vocabulary instead of upgraded wording.
