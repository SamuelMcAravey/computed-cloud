# Cadence Notes

- The pacing is functional. The writer usually opens with orientation, gives just enough context, then moves to the ask.
- The voice tolerates roughness. A sentence can widen mid-thought if the extra detail matters.
- Fragments are acceptable when they feel useful. Roughly 26% of sentences in the selected examples are short enough to behave like near-fragments.
- Paragraphs are short and purposeful, usually one idea per paragraph.
- Repetition is used for emphasis only in small doses, often around timing, uncertainty, or the one thing still needed.
- Spoken-language carryover is normal. The writing does not try to sound literary or perfectly compressed.
