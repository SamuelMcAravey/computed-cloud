# Openings And Closings

## Greetings

- `Hi [name],`
- `Prezados,`

## Sentence Starters

- `Hi [name], [child] and [child] [family] had an unplanned family event that did not allow them to attend today.`
- `Even if there is no availability that week, I would like to come in to collect a copy of my records.`
- `Support key: [id]`
- `I’ve attached the transcripts that arrived today for [company].`
- `I hope you're having a good week.`
- `Morning again.`
- `Good morning.`
- `Morning, how’s it going?`

## Bridge Phrases

- `just wanted to`
- `please let me know`
- `i hope`
- `would you`
- `i wanted to`

## Signoffs

- `- [sender]`
- `[sender]`
- `[phone]`
- `Best regards,`
- `Atenciosamente,`

## Notes

- Requests are often sharpened with a concrete question rather than a rhetorical flourish.
- Uncertainty is usually expressed as trying to understand what changed or what the best next step is.
- Closings stay short. A plain `Thanks,` or `Best regards,` is more common than a fully polished ending.
