# Revision Rules

- Get to the point within the first two sentences.
- Keep the message anchored in one real situation, not a generic summary.
- Prefer plain words over upgraded synonyms.
- Preserve small rough edges if they sound natural; do not sand everything down into perfect symmetry.
- If the draft is too sharp, soften it with one practical hedge, not a paragraph of apology.
- If the draft is too vague, add one concrete detail or one direct question.
- Keep closings short.
- Do not add fake warmth, recruiter-speak, or executive-summary framing.
- When revising for this voice, cut abstraction first and polish second.
