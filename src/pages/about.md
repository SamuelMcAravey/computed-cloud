# About

Computed Cloud is my place to document practical engineering work in enterprise systems. It exists to capture real constraints around payments, compliance workflows, and integrations that live longer than a product cycle. The focus is on what ships, what breaks, and the changes that keep systems reliable over time. I write for engineers and founders who have to balance correctness, operational risk, and delivery speed in B2B SaaS.

My writing style is practical, implementation-focused, and opinionated about reliability and maintainability.

## What you'll find here

- Enterprise integrations with ERP and finance systems
- Payments and compliance workflows that must be correct and auditable
- Multi-tenant design choices and their operational impact
- Data-intensive system design tradeoffs
- Reliability engineering and incident response patterns
- Observability practices that shorten feedback loops
- Pragmatic modernization without big-bang rewrites
- CI/CD automation that improves release confidence
- Infrastructure decisions that support high availability
- Platform case studies with clear constraints and outcomes
- Tooling notes that remove repetitive engineering work
- Open-source writeups when the code is worth sharing

## My background (short version)

I'm Samuel McAravey, a strategic engineering leader based in Billings, MT. I have over 16 years of experience building and scaling engineering organizations and platforms, primarily in B2B SaaS with payment and compliance constraints. My work is grounded in data-intensive systems and practical delivery at scale.

At PayeWaive, I led technology and engineering as CTO/Head of Engineering for a multi-tenant B2B SaaS platform on Azure. The platform was built to be geo-redundant and achieved 99.99% uptime for mission-critical payment and compliance workflows. We integrated with ERP systems like Viewpoint Vista and Spectrum, and I built a metrics-driven engineering culture for a globally distributed team. Over time, we improved delivery speed and reduced defects, including through an AI-powered SDLC.

At Nvoicepay (Corpay), I led development teams and modernization work for a high-volume B2B payments platform. That work included CI/CD automation, improving release and rollback outcomes, and delivering high-availability, geo-redundant Azure infrastructure. I also supported international payments initiatives and early Kubernetes rollout work. Outside software, I co-founded Pearl Bakery and built a custom internal ERP/MRP that handled orders through production planning, bill of materials, packing, invoicing, and accounting integration, with iPad-based workflows on the production floor.

## How I think about engineering

- Reliability is a feature because trust is the product for payments workflows.
- Incremental modernization beats big-bang rewrites because operations and revenue do not pause.
- Integrations are product work because adoption depends on existing systems.
- Auditability matters because compliance is only as good as its evidence trail.
- High availability is a design choice because recovery is always more expensive than prevention.
- Automation should target repeat pain because it compounds over time.
- Observability should answer questions fast because incident time is expensive.
- Clear ownership wins because ambiguity breaks on-call response.
