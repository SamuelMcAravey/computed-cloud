# About

Computed Cloud is where I document practical engineering work in enterprise systems: the messy parts, the tradeoffs, and the patterns that survive production. I write for builders who live with integrations, payment workflows, compliance constraints, and long-lived systems. The goal is to share how things actually behave, not just how they look in a demo. The posts are based on real operational experience in B2B SaaS and data-intensive platforms.

My writing style is practical, implementation-focused, and opinionated about reliability and maintainability.

## What you’ll find here

- Enterprise integrations that involve real constraints and real downstream systems
- Payments and compliance workflows that must be correct, auditable, and durable
- Multi-tenant platform design decisions and their operational impact
- Modernization approaches that respect risk, uptime, and existing revenue
- Reliability, uptime, and incident response lessons from production systems
- Observability practices that help teams find the truth quickly
- CI/CD habits that reduce rollout risk and shorten feedback loops
- Tooling and automation that remove repetitive work for engineers
- Case studies from platform work with clear constraints and outcomes
- Notes on building teams that can ship while staying on-call healthy
- Open-source writeups when the code is worth sharing

## My background (short version)

I’m Samuel McAravey, a strategic engineering leader based in Billings, MT. I’ve spent over 16 years building and scaling engineering organizations and platforms, with a focus on B2B SaaS, payments/compliance workflows, and data-intensive systems.

At PayeWaive, I led technology and engineering for a multi-tenant B2B SaaS platform in construction tech, built on Azure. The system was designed for geo-redundancy and delivered 99.99% uptime for mission-critical payment and compliance workflows. We integrated with ERP platforms like Viewpoint Vista and Spectrum, while running a metrics-driven engineering culture that improved productivity and reduced time-to-market for new client requirements.

At Nvoicepay (Corpay), I helped modernize a 50+ person engineering organization and led teams supporting a high-volume payments platform processing billions annually. That work included CI/CD automation, release improvements, and high-availability infrastructure in Azure, plus international payments initiatives and early Kubernetes rollout work. Outside of software, I co-founded Pearl Bakery and built an internal ERP/MRP system that ran the business: orders to production planning, bill of materials, packing, invoicing, and accounting integration, with iPad-based workflows on the shop floor.

## How I think about engineering

- Reliability is a feature because users trust outcomes more than features.
- Incremental modernization beats big-bang rewrites because revenue and operations keep moving.
- Integrations are product work because they shape how customers actually adopt your system.
- Auditability matters because payments and compliance workflows are only as strong as their evidence.
- Automation should target repeat pain because it is the fastest way to reduce cognitive load.
- Operational metrics must be honest because teams can only improve what they can see.
- High availability is a design choice because outage recovery is always more expensive than prevention.
- Clear ownership wins because ambiguity is the root of most on-call failures.
